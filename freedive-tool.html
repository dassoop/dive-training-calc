training program

Phase 1 — Rebuild the Base (Weeks 1-4)

Goal: Retrain CO2 tolerance. Get comfortable holding your breath again.

Daily Breath Work (10-15 min, lying down)

Diaphragmatic Breathing — 5 min
Lie flat. Inhale through nose (4 sec, belly rises). Exhale through mouth (6-8 sec). Longer exhale activates parasympathetic system.

Beginner CO2 Table
Take a relaxed breath, hold until first urge = your baseline. Do 5 rounds at 50% baseline with 2:00 rest. Retest every 3-4 days.

Recovery Breathing
After every hold: sharp inhale, hold 2 sec, exhale. Repeat 3x. Hook breathing — make it automatic.

Body Work (3x/week)

Rib cage stretches, squats 3x15, calf raises 3x20, walking lunges 2x10. Aerobic: 20-30 min walk/jog, nose breathing only.

Milestones
  • Establish baseline (day 1)
  • Comfortable CO2 table at 50% (week 1)
  • Baseline reaches 1:30+ (week 2-3)
  • CO2 table at 60-70% (week 3-4)

Phase 2 — Water Reintroduction (Weeks 3-6)

Goal: Activate mammalian dive reflex. Build water comfort.

Pool Sessions (1x/week, with buddy)

Face-In Static
Shallow end, float face down. Dive reflex only triggers with face submerged. Build to matching dry times.

Dynamic Apnea
Push off wall, streamline, glide. Slow hip kicks. Start: 1 pool length (25m).

Duck Dive
Pike at surface, legs straight up. One clean kick, streamline down.

Breathe-Up
2-3 min calm breathing before every hold. Last breath: belly, chest, top off gently.

Milestones
  • Comfortable face-in holds (session 1)
  • Wet static matches dry static (week 4-5)
  • 1 pool length underwater (week 4)
  • Smooth duck dive (week 5-6)

Phase 3 — Ocean Ready (Weeks 6-8+)

Goal: Confident shallow ocean dives. Bottom time before depth.

Equalization (Frenzel)

Pinch nose, close mouth, push back of tongue upward like "T" or "K." Practice dry: 20 reps daily. Equalize every few feet — don't wait for pain.

Ocean Sessions

Start at 10-15 ft. Build bottom time before depth. 45 sec at 15 ft beats touching 30 ft and bolting up. Hunting comes last.

Milestones
  • Frenzel to 10-15 ft (week 6-7)
  • 30 sec bottom time at 15 ft (week 7-8)
  • 3-dive series with proper recovery (week 8)
  • First hunt-ready session (week 8+)

Technique Reference

CO2 Tolerance

CO2 builds up when you hold your breath. Your brain reads it as "suffocating" but you have minutes of O2 left. CO2 tables train your body to delay the panic response. Single biggest factor in longer holds.

Mammalian Dive Reflex

Face in cold water triggers: heart rate drops 10-25%, blood shifts to core/brain, spleen releases extra red blood cells. Why wet holds beat dry holds.

Hook Breathing

After surfacing: sharp inhale, hold 2 sec, exhale. 3x. Keeps O2-rich blood flowing to brain during the critical 15 seconds post-surface. Prevents blackout.

Frenzel Equalization

Tongue acts as piston. Nose pinched, airway closed, push back of tongue up. Compresses air into eustachian tubes. Practice with "T" or "K" sounds.

Duck Dive

Bend sharply at waist, head/arms straight down, legs vertical. Body weight drives you under. One fin kick when fins submerge. Streamline forward. Glide.

Safety — Non-Negotiable

  • Never hold your breath in water alone
  • Never hyperventilate before a hold (3+ fast breaths = dangerous)
  • Always hook breathe after surfacing
  • Tingling, tunnel vision, dizziness = stop immediately
  • One up, one down — always a buddy watching from surface
